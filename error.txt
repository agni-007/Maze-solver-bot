Maze will be having physical walls
Maze size - 8ft*8ft
Track width is 23cm.
Wall height will be maximum 10cm.
Each team will get 3 runs, best of 3 will be taken for ranking.
A total of 12 mins will be allotted for each team, where each attempt spanning 4 mins.
Unlike conventional maze solvers , this competition will have only a single path from start to end , there will be no dead ends , decision based junctions etc, your bot must travel through the entire maze track in the shortest time possible without hitting or touching the walls.
Track will be divided into 8 sections and there will be equal points for each section.
If your bot hits a wall then points till the preceding section will be awarded.
Speed will be the sole criteria of winning, ranking will be based on section points awarded and for same section points fastest bot will be placed higher in ranking.
Bot must start at the designated starting point which will be the top left corner and finish at the finishing point which will be the bottom right corner.
No human contact with bot once run begins — immediate disqualification 
Once the competition run begins, the bot must operate fully autonomously without any human intervention. Remote or wireless control of any kind is strictly prohibited. 
Teams may have the option of taking 10 mins of reprogramming time in between with penalty points applicable.

All participating teams must have individual bots; no sharing of bots will be allowed.
All scoring and penalty criteria will be shared 2 weeks before the competition.
The decision of the organising committee shall be final and binding in all matters.
